National Security & Defense

National security ensures the protection of a country's citizens, economy, and institutions from external and internal threats. It involves defense strategies, cybersecurity, intelligence operations, and law enforcement to maintain stability and sovereignty.

1. Cybersecurity

Protecting critical infrastructure, government networks, and sensitive data from cyber threats, hacking, and espionage.

2. Border Security

Monitoring and controlling the movement of people and goods across national borders to prevent illegal activities and threats.

3. Military Defense

Maintaining armed forces, defense systems, and strategic deterrence to safeguard national sovereignty and interests.

4. Intelligence and Counterterrorism

Gathering intelligence, preventing terrorist activities, and responding to security threats through national agencies.

5. Economic Security

Ensuring financial stability, preventing economic espionage, and protecting key industries from foreign influence.

6. Space Security

Protecting satellites, space assets, and communications from potential threats, including cyber and kinetic attacks.

7. Energy Security

Securing the supply of energy resources, power grids, and fuel reserves to maintain national stability.

8. Public Health Security

Preparing for and responding to biological threats, pandemics, and bioterrorism to protect public well-being.

9. Environmental Security

Addressing climate change, natural disasters, and resource management to prevent conflicts and instability.

10. Transportation and Infrastructure Security

Protecting critical transportation networks, communication systems, and public infrastructure from sabotage and attacks.
